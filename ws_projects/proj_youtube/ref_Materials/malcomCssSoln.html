The Problem: Noise in Light Transport Simulation

  • Noise is a major issue in light transport simulation, making photorealistic scenes difficult to achieve.
  • Noise is caused by inaccuracies in estimating light rays and bouncing, taking millions of rays to simulate.
  • Current methods take hours to weeks to finish, making real-time light transport seem impossible.

Finding a Solution: Importance Sampling and Smart Algorithms

  • Importance sampling: choosing where to shoot rays in the scene.
  • Metropolis Light Transport and Manifold Exploration algorithms are examples of smart algorithms.

The Breakthrough: Voxel Cone Tracing

  • 2011 paper by NVIDIA: Voxel Cone Tracing.
  • Subdivides the space into voxels, reducing the complexity of light simulation.
  • Only computes 2 bounces for each light ray.

The Future: Ray Allocation and Denoising

  • Ray allocation: allocating ray samples more effectively.
  • Denoising algorithms: removing noise from simulated light.

Real-Time Light Transport Achieved?

  • Paper 2: rendering an amusement park scene with 20 million triangles in milliseconds.
  • Interactive method, 10-100 times faster than previous techniques.
